## Glossary

LED
: Light emitting diodes convert energy to light

Electricity
: flow of free electrons between atoms

Copper
: conductor has a lot of free electrons

Rubber
: insulator that holds it's electrons tightly


Electron Flow
- electrons flow from `neg -> pos`
- flow `- .... +`

Conventional Flow
- model where electrons flow from `pos -> neg`
- electron flow is the actual process
- flow `+ .... -`
- traditional model, but, does not describe the underlying process

Conductor
- has 1-3 electrons in valence shell
- conduction band is close / overlaps valence

Insulator
- conduction band is packed fully

Semi-conductor
- 1 too many electrons in outer shell
- valence band has 1 extra electron
- with extra energy valence electron is promoted to conduction band

Doping 
- creat a semi-conductor
- mix other material in to provide extra electrons
- 2 types of doping `n-type` and `p-type`

P-type
- "hole" exists 
- accept electrons type 
- 1 fewer electron 
- positively charged 
- receive electrons

N-type
- 1 extra electron 
- negatively charged 
- give electrons

p-n Junction
- a barrier forms between p & n materials
- the barrier has a voltage of 0.7 V
- if electrons come to the cathode with greater voltage then they can jump the barrier
- jump barrier is "forward bias"

- material has mix of stable atoms with another atom that has odd number electrons

Diode
- ref [diodes explained](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Fwj_d3uO5g8)
- construction is `anode .... p-type | n-type .... cathode`
- current can only flow in a single direction `p -> n`
- acts as either conductor or insulator
- symbol `-->|--` or `--|<--`
- the arrow points in direction of conventional flow
- the pipe is the stripe
- note stripe is usally on the `n-type` side
- creates a voltage drop in any circuit

Forward Bias - Diode as conductor
- electron flow occurs
- connect `neg` term to `stripe`
- aka Forward Bias

Reverse Bias - Diode as insulator 
- no electron flow
- connect `pos` to `stripe`
- aka Reverse Bias

Diode uses
- convert ac -> dc
- guard components
- full bridge rectifier

LED 
- longer leg is p-type
- shorter is n-type

## Notes

Bulbs,
- convert energy to 95% heat, 5% light


... by mixing these types we can create a semi-conductor

... energy flows from high density -> low density


Semi-conductor
- mix `n-type` and `p-type` material in such a way that electron flow is controlled
- given `+ ____ n p ____ -` when current flows n and p electrons repel each other
- given `- ____ n p ____ +` when current flows electrons flow from `n -> p`
